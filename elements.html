Paneer

Boil the Milk: Pour the milk into a heavy-bottomed pan and bring it to a boil over medium heat. Stir occasionally to prevent the milk from sticking to the bottom of the pan.
Add Acid: Once the milk comes to a boil, reduce the heat to low. Add the lemon juice or white vinegar gradually while stirring continuously. You will start to see the milk solids (curds) separating from the whey. If the separation doesn't occur, add a little more lemon juice or vinegar.
Separate Curds and Whey: Once the curds have completely separated from the whey, turn off the heat. Let it sit for a few minutes to allow more curds to form.
Strain the Curds: Place a cheesecloth or a thin cotton cloth over a strainer and position it over a bowl or in the sink. Pour the curdled milk through the cheesecloth to separate the curds from the whey. The whey will drain off, leaving the curds in the cheesecloth.
Rinse the Curds: Rinse the curds under cold running water to remove any traces of lemon juice or vinegar. This helps in preserving the taste of the paneer.
Tie and Hang the Paneer (Optional): Bundle up the cheesecloth containing the paneer and hang it for about 30 minutes to let excess water drain out. This step is optional; you can also press the paneer directly.
Press the Paneer: Place the bundled paneer on a flat surface and press it with a heavyweight (like a heavy pan or a can) for about 1-2 hours. This helps in shaping the paneer into a block.
Cut and Use: Once the paneer is set, you can cut it into cubes or slices as per your requirement. It is now ready to be used in various recipes.
